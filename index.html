Running day

Try to run first thing in the morning before your day gets busy. Or, run during your lunch break. Look for run clubs and running meetups in your area for support and motivation. Do short runs during the week, and save your long runs for the weekends when you have more time.

Keep it simple “Establishing a new habit is easier if you start with a simple change, then build in complexity over time,” says sport and exercise psychologist Helen O’Connor (@psycurious). “For the first few mornings, set your alarm for 6am, get up, put your kit on and walk or run around the block. Once you’re used to the early start, build up the distance.”
Experiment with food Some people wake up hungry, in which case a small snack – a banana or half a piece of toast and peanut butter – should see you through a short run. Others find they don’t need anything except a glass of water. A 2010 study showed that running in a fasted state (before breakfast) helps maintain a healthy waistline more than running after breakfast.
Lay out your kit “Instead of having your slippers by the bed, lay out your running kit – trainers, socks, trousers, top and jacket,” says Helen. Once you’re dressed in it, there’s no going back, but if you shuffle downstairs in your slippers and PJs, the temptation to crawl back under the duvet is too big. Ensure that your kit is warm and hi-vis, so that you can be seen by cars, cyclists and pedestrians.
